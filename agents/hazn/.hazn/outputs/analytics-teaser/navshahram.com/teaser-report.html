Security & Infrastructure

Security Grade: F — Zero Headers Detected

Zero HTTP security headers were found on navshahram.com. This is a significant vulnerability signal for both visitors and search engines. Google's Safe Browsing and browser security prompts rely on these headers.

🔴 No Strict-Transport-Security (HSTS)
Without HSTS, browsers won't enforce HTTPS on repeat visits. Attackers can downgrade connections. Fix: Add Strict-Transport-Security: max-age=31536000; includeSubDomains in LiteSpeed config.
🔴 No X-Frame-Options
The site can be embedded in an iframe — enabling clickjacking attacks. This is a 5-minute LiteSpeed fix.
🔴 No X-Content-Type-Options
Without this header, browsers may MIME-sniff responses — a known XSS vector. Should be set to nosniff.
🔴 No Referrer-Policy or Permissions-Policy
Visitor data is leaked to third-party sites via the Referer header. Camera/microphone permissions are unrestricted.
✅ HTTPS enforced via 301 redirect
HTTP requests correctly redirect to HTTPS. SSL certificate is active. This is the baseline — now the remaining headers need adding.

PIPEDA Compliance Risk: Facebook Pixel fires without consent
Canada's PIPEDA and Quebec's Law 25 require explicit consent before firing tracking pixels. No cookie consent banner was detected on navshahram.com. The Facebook Pixel (PixelYourSite) fires on every page load without user consent — this creates legal exposure, especially for Quebec visitors.
⚠️ Duplicate SEO Plugins: Yoast + Rank Math Both Active
Both Yoast SEO and Rank Math are active simultaneously. This generates duplicate title tags, duplicate meta descriptions, and duplicate schema markup in the HTML — sending conflicting signals to Google. One of these must be deactivated and its settings migrated to the other. This is a critical SEO issue dressed as a MarTech problem.

Copy Audit

Copy Grade: C+ — Honest but Generic

Nav Shahram's copy leads with sincerity: "My approach is simple: loyalty, honesty, integrity, and service come first." That's admirable — but every realtor in Canada says the same thing. The site doesn't differentiate Nav in a crowded market.

⚠️
Hero Headline: Descriptive, not magnetic
"Local Full time Realtor with Over 10 Years of Experience" is a factual statement, not a value proposition. It tells visitors what Nav is, not why that matters to them. Better: "Coquitlam's most referred realtor — 10 years, hundreds of families, one goal: yours." (Uses social proof + local + specificity.)
🔴
CTA Copy: "Browse" and "Search" — conversion killers
The primary CTAs are "Browse" and "Search" — passive, generic, and interchangeable with any MLS portal. High-converting real estate CTAs are outcomes-focused: "See homes in Coquitlam", "Get my home's value", "Book a free strategy call". The current CTAs have no urgency and no differentiation.
⚠️
Value Proposition: Too abstract, not quantified
"Loyalty, honesty, integrity" are table stakes — not differentiators. What's Nav's average days-on-market vs. market average? Sale-price-to-list-price ratio? Number of families helped? These numbers convert. "Ranked top 10% of Greater Vancouver realtors" is mentioned in meta tags but not prominently in the page body.
✅
Social Proof: Logos visible, testimonials absent above fold
RE/MAX, CREA, and other logos are visible — good credibility signals. However, no text testimonials appear in the visible fold. A single powerful testimonial with name, photo, and outcome (e.g., "Nav sold our home in 8 days for $23K over asking") would significantly increase trust-to-click conversion.
UX Audit

UX Grade: B− — Clean but Passive

The Salient theme delivers a professional, visually coherent experience. Navigation is clear, hierarchy is logical, and mobile responsiveness is functional. The UX gap is strategic: the site is designed to inform, not convert. There's no friction reduction for the primary user action (booking a consultation).

⚠️
Above-fold CTA is weak: "Browse" and "Search" don't create urgency
The immediate scroll view contains the hero headline and two buttons. Neither button drives toward a consultation. The highest-value action (talking to Nav) is buried at the bottom in a "Have any questions? Get in touch" section.
⚠️
No sticky navigation CTA on mobile
Mobile users who scroll past the hero have no persistent path back to a conversion action. A sticky "Call Nav" or "Book Free Consultation" button in the mobile header would recover users who are interested but passive.
✅
Visual hierarchy is logical and professional
The Salient theme's section-based layout creates clear content blocks: selling, buying, process, about, trust logos, contact. The red brand color creates visual consistency. Typography is readable at all sizes.
⚠️
Mobile viewport locked with user-scalable=0
The meta viewport tag includes user-scalable=0 and maximum-scale=1. This prevents users from zooming in on text — a WCAG accessibility violation that also reduces usability for older visitors and may impact Google's accessibility assessment.
CRO Audit

CRO Grade: C — Leaking Qualified Leads

The site gets visitors. It doesn't convert them efficiently. Three structural CRO problems are costing Nav qualified consultations every week: weak CTAs at decision moments, no social proof near conversion points, and a contact form that creates friction instead of reducing it.

🔴
No conversion event above 50% scroll depth
The primary conversion action — contacting Nav — doesn't appear until the very bottom of the page ("Have any questions? Get in touch"). Users who leave before scrolling 80%+ never encounter a conversion opportunity. Estimated conversion leakage: 40-60% of qualified visitors.
🔴
Social proof not adjacent to CTAs
Trust logos (RE/MAX, CREA) appear at the bottom — far from the decision points. In conversion psychology, social proof must appear within 1-2 scroll positions of the CTA. Adding a "Google 5★ · 47 reviews" badge next to the contact button would lift conversions 15-25%.
⚠️
Contact Form 7 with no friction reduction
Contact Form 7 is functional but plain. No reassurance copy ("I typically respond within 2 hours"), no form field validation UX, no calendar booking integration. Replacing with a Calendly embed or adding a phone CTA alongside the form would reduce friction at the final conversion step.
⚠️
No A/B testing or conversion tracking infrastructure
Without GTM, there's no clean way to track form submissions as conversion events in GA4. Without conversion data, there's no basis for A/B testing. The MarTech gap directly limits CRO capability.
