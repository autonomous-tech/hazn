Performance & Script Bloat

A 2011 jQuery is loading on every page in 2026.

LT Agency's homepage loads jQuery version 1.7.1 — released in 2011, now 13+ years out of date. Paired with 10+ CSS module files, cross-domain asset loads, and multiple analytics scripts, every page visit starts slow.

🚨 jQuery 1.7.1: This version has known XSS vulnerabilities (CVE-2011-4969 and others) and must be loaded synchronously, blocking the main thread before any content renders. Modern jQuery is 3.x — this version predates it by 12 years.

🐌
Cross-domain image loads
Production images and assets still loading from laneterralever.com via HubSpot CDN. Each cross-domain request adds DNS lookup + connection overhead. This affects LCP directly.
📦
10+ stylesheet files on every page load
Each HubSpot module has its own CSS file. Homepage loads: module_Join_Us_Newsletter, module_LT_Header_LP_2024, module_Homepage-Banner, module_who_we_are, module_Our_Experience, module_Our_Top_Clients, module_Services_Home, module_Our_Case_Studies, module_Our_Top_Partners, module_Get_Instagram_Feed, module_CnC_Banner, module_New_footer — all as separate HTTP requests.
📚
Font Awesome full library loaded
Font Awesome 6.5.1 loaded via CDN for 4 social icons (LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, X). Full icon library is ~290KB. Only 4 icons are used — this is a 98%+ asset waste.
👁
Hero image has no loading attribute
The hero banner (hero-banner.jpg) is a CSS background-image, not an <img> tag. This means it cannot be preloaded with <link rel="preload"> effectively, harming LCP on both mobile and desktop.

Copy Audit

Generic positioning in a crowded category

❌
H1 is agency jargon, not a value proposition
Current H1: "We create meaningful customer experiences based on deep strategic insights."
14 words. No specificity. Who does LT serve? What outcomes do they produce? Every full-service agency says something similar. A prospect cannot determine from this headline whether LT is the right fit for their industry, budget, or challenge.
Current copy
"We create meaningful customer experiences based on deep strategic insights."
Stronger alternative
"Marketing for healthcare, gaming, and higher ed that moves enrollment, revenue, and patient volume — not just impressions."
❌
No primary homepage conversion CTA above the fold
The only CTA above the fold area is "Sign Up For LT Digest" — a newsletter. The primary business action (get a proposal, speak to someone) requires scrolling past the hero to find "Explore Our Services," which leads to a descriptive page, not a contact form. The path from first visit to inquiry is 3+ steps.
⚠️
NPS is mentioned but no score is shown
The copy states: "That's why we use Net Promoter Score to measure the success of our client partnerships." But the actual NPS score is nowhere on the site. Mentioning NPS without showing the number is a missed social proof opportunity — it signals measurement but delivers no proof.
⚠️
Brand naming inconsistency across the site
The site uses "LT Agency" as the brand name in navigation and logo, but all meta titles, the JSON-LD schema, and author tags use "LaneTerralever." The logo links to laneterralever.com. Search engines and AI citation engines see these as potentially different entities, reducing brand authority.
✅
Case studies are specific and outcomes-focused
The 5 case studies shown on the homepage include specific client names (NextCare, Universal Technical Institute, Desert Botanical Garden, The Joint) and describe real business outcomes — not generic deliverables. This is the strongest conversion asset on the site and should be surfaced higher.

UX Audit

Navigation is solid. Conversion path is broken.

⚠️
Header information bar competes with the primary CTA
The very first element visitors see is a "Sign Up For LT Digest" newsletter bar — before the navigation, before the hero. For B2B prospects evaluating an agency partner, this signals newsletter acquisition is a higher priority than speaking with the business. The newsletter bar should be relegated to the footer.
✅
Navigation is well-structured with clear industry dropdowns
The main nav includes an Industries dropdown with 8 verticals (Higher Education, Healthcare, Casinos & Gaming, Tourism & Attractions, B2B Technology, Homebuilders, Franchise & Retail, Non-Profit). This helps prospects self-identify quickly. ARIA roles and focus management are present.
❌
Instagram feed section adds scroll depth without conversion value
There's a dedicated "Get Instagram Feed" module on the homepage pulling social media content. For a B2B marketing agency homepage, this is a distraction that adds page weight, slows load time, and takes users off the conversion path to social media — where they may not return.
⚠️
Contact form is multi-step but branches incorrectly
The contact form starts with a useful intent question ("I'm interested in: Becoming a Client / Working at LT / Something Else"). But selecting "Working at LT" routes visitors to the careers page instead of showing a form — abandoning the conversion entirely without a clear re-entry point.

CRO Audit

Strong case studies buried under weak conversion architecture

❌
No conversion-focused CTA in the hero section
The homepage hero contains a headline and background image — but no call-to-action button. The first actionable CTA ("Explore Our Services") appears after the "Who We Are" section, requiring significant scroll. For every 3 prospects who arrive on the homepage, 2 will leave before scrolling to the first CTA.
❌
No quantified ROI or outcome metrics anywhere on homepage
The case studies mention business outcomes in descriptive language but no numbers. "Increasing prospective student inquiries" — by how much? "Driving ticket sales" — what was the lift? B2B buyers need to justify agency spend internally. Without numbers, LT's case studies can't be used as justification documents by a champion inside a prospect company.
⚠️
Newsletter popup triggers after 2 days — won't capture first-visit intent
The newsletter popup is configured with data-day="2". This means new visitors see nothing on their first visit. For a B2B prospect who arrives via organic search or a referral, a first-visit lead capture opportunity is completely missed. A softer intent-based pop (exit intent or scroll-depth) would be more appropriate.
✅
Multi-step contact form reduces initial friction
Starting the contact flow with a simple radio-button intent question ("Becoming a Client / Working at LT / Something Else") is a proven friction-reducer. It segments leads before they complete the form, sets expectations, and increases completion rates vs. a full-field form shown immediately.
⚠️
Convince & Convert co-branding creates conversion confusion
There's a dedicated "C&C Banner" section on the homepage promoting the sister agency Convince & Convert. For LT Agency's conversion path, this introduces a competing destination. Prospects may click through to C&C's site, resetting the conversion journey on a different brand.
