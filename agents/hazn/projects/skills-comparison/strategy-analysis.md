# Strategy & Growth Skills Analysis
## HAZN vs MarketingSkills Comparison

*Analysis Date: January 2025*
*Focus: What a B2B website agency can learn from marketing-focused skills*

---

## Executive Summary

**HAZN** is strong at *building* — we have the technical and design execution skills to create high-quality B2B websites. **MarketingSkills** is strong at *growing* — they understand the psychology, strategy, and channels that drive traffic and conversions.

The key insight: **We build the house, they know how to fill it with visitors.**

---

## 1. Overlap Analysis

### Where We Cover the Same Ground

| Area | HAZN Approach | MarketingSkills Approach |
|------|---------------|--------------------------|
| **Conversion optimization** | UX patterns, wireframe flows, frontend design that guides users | Psychology triggers, pricing presentation, churn flows |
| **B2B context** | B2B-specific UX and wireframing | SaaS/B2B marketing context throughout |
| **User psychology** | Implicit in UX best practices | Explicit via marketing-psychology skill |
| **Lead generation** | Forms, CTAs in wireframes | Free tools, paid ads, referral programs |

### The Critical Difference

- **We optimize the experience** of users who arrive
- **They optimize the flow** of users who arrive AND the acquisition of new users

**Overlap Score: ~20%** — We work on different parts of the same funnel.

---

## 2. Their Advantages (What We're Missing)

### 🔴 Critical Gaps

#### **Pricing Strategy**
We build pricing pages, but do we truly understand:
- Decoy pricing psychology
- Annual vs monthly framing
- Feature packaging that maximizes perceived value
- Free trial vs freemium decision frameworks

**Impact on our work:** We design pricing pages based on aesthetics and UX patterns, not pricing psychology. This is a missed opportunity.

#### **Marketing Psychology**
Mental models that drive decisions:
- Loss aversion in copy and CTAs
- Social proof placement and timing
- Urgency/scarcity frameworks
- Anchoring in feature comparisons

**Impact on our work:** Our wireframes and copy lack intentional psychological triggers. We rely on "best practices" without understanding *why* they work.

#### **Launch Strategy**
Product launch playbooks:
- Pre-launch list building
- Announcement sequencing
- Beta/waitlist psychology
- Launch day conversion tactics

**Impact on our work:** We deliver websites, but clients often don't know how to launch them effectively. We could add massive value here.

### 🟡 Moderate Gaps

#### **Free Tool Strategy**
Interactive calculators and tools as lead magnets:
- ROI calculators
- Assessment tools
- Comparison widgets
- Diagnostic quizzes

**Impact on our work:** We have the technical skills to build these (Next.js is perfect for interactive tools) but lack the strategic framework for *which* tools to build and *how* to position them.

#### **Paid Ads Knowledge**
Understanding ad creative and campaign structure:
- Landing page requirements for different ad platforms
- Message match between ads and pages
- Platform-specific conversion patterns

**Impact on our work:** We build landing pages without considering ad platform requirements. Pages optimized for organic might underperform in paid campaigns.

#### **Churn Prevention**
Post-conversion optimization:
- Cancel flow design
- Save offer UX
- Dunning email sequences

**Impact on our work:** We rarely consider the "exit experience" in our UX work. Cancel flows are often an afterthought.

### 🟢 Minor Gaps (Nice to Have)

- **Referral Programs:** Could inform how we design referral UX
- **Ad Creative:** Mostly execution-focused, less relevant to web development
- **Marketing Ideas (140 tactics):** Useful for ideation, not core to our work

## 3. Our Advantages (What We Have That They Don't)

### 🟢 Major Strengths

They strategize, we ship.
- **Payload + Next.js stack:** Full implementation capability
- **Frontend design patterns:** Production-ready code approaches

**Why it matters:** Strategy without execution is just a document. We turn strategy into real websites.

- Enterprise-friendly information architecture
- B2B-specific social proof patterns (case studies, logos, testimonials)

**Why it matters:** MarketingSkills appears SaaS-focused but less deeply specialized in B2B website UX patterns.

#### **Wireframing Discipline**
Visual planning before build:
- Page-level structure thinking
- Component-based design system approach
- Client communication through tangible artifacts

**Why it matters:** We can prototype and validate before expensive implementation. Marketing skills are mostly conceptual.

#### **CMS Architecture**

- Flexible page builders
- Content modeling

## 4. Learning Opportunities (What We Should Adopt)

### Priority 1: Create a Pricing Page Skill

**What to include:**
- Pricing psychology principles (anchoring, decoys, framing)
- SaaS pricing page patterns by company stage
- Feature comparison table best practices
- FAQ section strategy for pricing pages
- Call-to-action psychology for different pricing tiers

**Action:** Research and create `pricing-page-strategy` skill. Reference MarketingSkills' pricing-strategy as foundation, adapt for web design context.

### Priority 2: Add Psychology Section to UX Reference

**What to add to b2b-ux-reference:**
- Loss aversion triggers in B2B contexts
- Social proof hierarchy (which proof types work when)
- Urgency patterns that don't feel sleazy
- Trust signal placement psychology

**Action:** Expand b2b-ux-reference with a "Conversion Psychology" section, ~500 words focused on B2B applications.

### Priority 3: Create Free Tool / Lead Magnet Skill

**What to include:**
- Types of interactive tools that work for B2B (calculators, assessments, comparators)
- Technical implementation patterns in Next.js
- Lead capture integration approaches
- Tool-to-demo conversion flow design

**Action:** Create `interactive-lead-tools` skill combining their strategy with our technical capability. This is a **competitive advantage** — we can both design AND build these.

### Priority 4: Launch Page Patterns

**What to include:**
- Pre-launch landing page wireframes
- Waitlist signup UX patterns
- Launch announcement page structures
- Post-launch social proof updates

**Action:** Add a "Launch Pages" section to b2b-wireframe skill, or create standalone `launch-page-patterns` skill.

### Priority 5: Ad-Optimized Landing Page Checklist

**What to include:**
- Message match requirements
- Above-fold optimization for paid traffic
- Platform-specific requirements (LinkedIn vs Google vs Meta)
- Speed requirements for paid campaigns

**Action:** Create a short checklist (~200 words) to reference when building landing pages intended for paid campaigns.

## 5. Our Strengths (Where We're Better Positioned)

### We're Stronger When...

| Scenario | Why We Win |
|----------|------------|
| **Client needs a complete website built** | We have full stack capability; they're strategy-only |
| **Complex B2B product with multiple personas** | Our B2B UX depth handles complexity better |
| **Enterprise clients** | Our wireframing and IA skills handle scale |
| **Technical integrations needed** | Payload + Next.js stack is production-ready |
| **Design system required** | Frontend patterns skill provides consistency |
| **Client has existing marketing team** | We complement their marketing with execution |

### They're Stronger When...

| Scenario | Why They Win |
|----------|--------------|
| **Client needs growth strategy** | Comprehensive marketing playbook |
| **Pricing/packaging decisions** | Dedicated pricing expertise |
| **Paid advertising guidance** | Campaign and creative knowledge |
| **Post-launch optimization** | Churn, referrals, retention focus |

### The Ideal Combination

**Best outcome:** HAZN skills for building, MarketingSkills thinking for conversion optimization layers.

A website built with:
- Our technical execution
- Our B2B UX patterns
- Their pricing psychology
- Their launch strategy

Would outperform either approach alone.
