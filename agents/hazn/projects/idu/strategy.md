## 1. Executive Summary

The International Democracy Union (IDU) is one of the world's most significant international political alliances — founded in 1983 by Thatcher, Reagan, and Kohl, with 80+ member parties across six continents. Yet its current website fails to reflect the organisation's historical weight, global reach, or institutional authority.

This redesign must close the gap between IDU's actual stature and its digital presence. The benchmark is not other political party websites — it is NATO, the European Parliament, and the Council of Europe. The goal is a site that commands the respect of foreign ministers, party chairmen, and international journalists within 3 seconds of loading.

**Core strategic mandate:** *Project authority. Enable engagement. Honour the institution.*

## 2. Organisational Analysis

### 2.1 What the IDU Actually Is

The IDU is not a campaign organisation or advocacy group — it is an **international institutional body** of the democratic centre-right. Its power derives from:

- **Founding legitimacy** — Created by the most consequential conservative leaders of the 20th century
- **Breadth of membership** — 80+ parties spanning every inhabited continent
- **Network value** — Direct connection between heads of government, party leaders, and international secretaries globally
- **Institutional continuity** — 40+ years of unbroken operation through the Cold War, post-communist transitions, Arab Spring, and democratic backsliding crises

The IDU's competitors for relevance are not other political unions — they are perceptions of irrelevance. The site must combat the assumption that the IDU is either inactive or of minor consequence.

### 2.2 Sub-Organisations

The IDU family of organisations represents significant breadth that the current site undersells:

| Sub-Org | Focus | Strategic Value |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| IYDU | International Young Democrat Union | Pipeline of next-gen leaders |
| IWDU | International Women's Democrat Union | Inclusion signal, gender balance |
| CDU | Caribbean Democrat Union | Regional depth |
| DUA | Democrat Union of Africa | Fastest-growing democratic continent |
| UPLA | Union of Latin American Parties | Major geopolitical region |
| APDU | Asia-Pacific Democrat Union | Strategic importance; fastest-growing economies |

These are not footnotes — they are proof of IDU's genuine global infrastructure.

### 2.3 Signature Assets

- **Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom** — Named for two of the most recognisable leaders of the 20th century. This award alone signals enormous institutional prestige.
- **Founding names** — Thatcher, Reagan, Kohl, Chirac (early meetings), Balladur — these are not just historical references; they are trust signals of the highest order.
- **Summits & forums** — Regular convening power at global scale is rare. It must be visible.

## 3. Audience Analysis

### 3.1 Primary Audiences

#### Audience 1: Member Party Leadership
*Chairmen, international secretaries, party executives*

**Goals:**
- Stay informed on IDU activities, resolutions, events
- Access member resources (login area, documents, directories)
- Ensure their party is properly represented on the site
- Connect with sister parties and IDU leadership

**What they need from the site:**
- Instant credibility (to show their party boards the IDU is prestigious)
- Easy access to news and events relevant to them
- A members' portal that works
- Their party logo/profile visible and correct

**Design implication:** The Members section must be searchable, filterable by region, and display each party with proper logos and information. The homepage must feel prestigious enough to screenshot and share in a board meeting.

---

#### Audience 2: Other Political Associations
*Sister organisations, affiliated international bodies, observer parties*

**Goals:**
- Understand IDU's scope and positioning
- Find contact information for collaboration
- Assess IDU's governance structure and legitimacy

**What they need from the site:**
- Clear About/governance section
- Regional sub-org information
- Contact routes

**Design implication:** Governance transparency matters. The Leadership page must be thorough.

---

#### Audience 3: Researchers & Think Tanks
*Academic political scientists, democracy researchers, policy institutes*

**Goals:**
- Verify membership data (which parties, when they joined)
- Access IDU resolutions, statements, and official positions
- Understand IDU's historical trajectory
- Find sources for citations

**What they need from the site:**
- Comprehensive, accurate Members database
- Archive of resolutions and statements
- Historical timeline (founding through present)
- Downloadable documents

**Design implication:** The News/Resolutions archive must be well-organised and searchable. Academic credibility = completeness and accuracy.

---

#### Audience 4: Governments & Diplomatic Contacts
*Foreign ministries, ambassadors, government advisers*

**Goals:**
- Understand IDU's position on global issues
- Find contact information for the Secretary General / leadership
- Assess IDU's legitimacy as a diplomatic interlocutor

**What they need from the site:**
- Authoritative tone and design (signals serious institution)
- Clear leadership and contact information
- Resolutions and statements on current issues
- Evidence of recent, active engagement

**Design implication:** The site's visual gravitas is itself a diplomatic signal. A poor site reflects on the institution's seriousness.

---

#### Audience 5: Media & Journalists
*International political correspondents, regional political press*

**Goals:**
- Find press releases and statements quickly
- Access high-resolution images and official photography
- Identify spokespeople and contact press office
- Verify IDU facts (founding, membership, etc.)

**What they need from the site:**
- Dedicated Media/Press section
- Downloadable press kit (logos, boilerplate, leadership photos)
- Quick-access to latest news and statements
- Clear press contact

**Design implication:** A proper Media Centre — not just a photo gallery — is essential. Journalists on deadline will not hunt for information.

---

### 3.2 Audience Priority Hierarchy

For the redesign, prioritise in this order:

1. **Media** — Their coverage multiplies IDU's reach; friction must be near-zero
2. **Member Party Leadership** — The institutional core; their experience reflects IDU's operational quality
3. **Governments** — Site's gravitas is a diplomatic asset
4. **Researchers** — Secondary but important for long-term credibility and citation
5. **Political Associations** — Lowest friction needed; they'll search deliberately

## 4. Competitive Landscape Analysis

### 4.1 Benchmark Analysis

| Institution | Strengths | Weaknesses | IDU Lessons |
|-------------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **NATO** (nato.int) | Authoritative tone, powerful imagery, clear news hierarchy, excellent search | Heavy, government-portal feel | Adopt: news prominence, search, gravitas. Avoid: bureaucratic complexity |
| **European Parliament** (europarl.europa.eu) | Multi-stakeholder, excellent document archive, member search | Language-heavy, complex navigation | Adopt: member directory design, document access. Avoid: over-complexity |
| **Council of Europe** (coe.int) | Strong treaty/document access, regional focus | Dated visual design | Adopt: institutional depth. Avoid: visual datedness |
| **EPP** (epp.eu) | Modern, political energy, strong manifesto framing | More partisan/campaign-feel than IDU needs | Adopt: member party grid, modern layout. Avoid: campaign urgency |

### 4.2 IDU's Unique Positioning

Unlike NATO or EPP, IDU operates across **all ideological variants of the democratic centre-right**, from libertarian-conservative to Christian democrat. This breadth is a strength — it makes IDU the only truly global democratic conservative network.

The site should assert: **"IDU is the world's only global alliance of centre-right democratic parties."**

This is a factual, defensible, powerful positioning statement.

## 5. Strategic Positioning

### 5.1 Positioning Statement (Internal)

> The IDU website is the digital headquarters of the world's premier alliance of democratic centre-right parties — a serious institutional presence that commands the respect of foreign ministers, serves the needs of 80+ member parties, and enables media and researchers to access information with ease.

### 5.2 Brand Voice

| Attribute | Definition | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Authoritative** | Statements of fact, not persuasion | "The IDU represents 80+ member parties across 6 continents" not "Join us!" |
| **Institutional** | Formal, measured, precise | Avoid exclamation marks, avoid casualness |
| **Historically grounded** | The past gives the IDU credibility | Reference to founding leaders where relevant |
| **Globally minded** | Every continent matters | Regional balance in imagery and copy |
| **Dignified** | Befitting a 40-year-old institution | No gimmicks, no viral bait |

### 5.3 Visual Brand Direction (within existing constraints)

The brief specifies keeping existing blues and logo. Within that:

- **Typography:** Use a serious serif or authoritative sans-serif for headings (similar to institutions like IMF or UN)
- **Photography:** Full-bleed summit photography, world leaders, formal settings — the visual language of international diplomacy
- **Colour palette:** IDU blues as primary; white for clarity; gold/warm accent for awards and prestige moments
- **Layout:** Clean, spacious, structured — not cluttered, not minimal-startup

## 6. Engagement Architecture (Conversion Strategy)

For institutional sites, "conversion" means **getting each audience to their goal with minimum friction**. Defined engagement KPIs by audience:

| Audience | Primary Engagement Goal | Secondary Goal |
|----------|------------------------|----------------|
| Media | Download press kit / find contact | Read latest statement |
| Member parties | Access member portal / find event | Find sister party |
| Governments | Read resolution / find Secretary General | Understand IDU scope |
| Researchers | Access members database / find document | Read historical info |
| Political orgs | Find contact / understand governance | Read about sub-orgs |

### 6.1 Trust Signal Architecture

Trust signals must appear **above the fold on the homepage** and recur throughout:

1. **Founding names** — "Founded by Thatcher, Reagan, and Kohl" in first 200 pixels
2. **Scale indicator** — "80+ member parties across 6 continents" immediately visible
3. **Recency signal** — Latest news/activity dated and prominent (proving IDU is active)
4. **Member logos** — Scrolling or grid display of recognisable party logos
5. **Bush-Thatcher Award** — Named for two world-historical figures; use it prominently
6. **Regional breadth** — Visual world map or continent icons showing global presence

## 7. Page-by-Page Strategy

### 7.1 Homepage

**Job:** Establish authority instantly; route each audience to their section.

**Structure:**
1. **Hero** — Full-bleed summit photography + positioning statement ("The Global Alliance of Centre-Right Democratic Parties") + founding line
2. **Stats bar** — 80+ member parties | 6 continents | Founded 1983 | [Current year active]
3. **Latest News** — 3 most recent news items with dates (proves activity)
4. **Upcoming Events** — Next 2-3 events with dates
5. **Member Parties** — Logo strip / map preview with CTA to full Members page
6. **About IDU** — Brief institutional description with CTA to About
7. **Bush-Thatcher Award** — Feature block (rotating past winners, prestige signal)
8. **Sub-organisations** — Grid of 6 regional/thematic bodies with logos
9. **Media** — Quick links: Press Kit, Latest Release, Press Contact

**Key decisions:**
- No carousel hero (carousels are ignored; single powerful image wins)
- No "Join Us" CTA — IDU is not recruiting; it's convening
- Date-stamped news is non-negotiable (proves the organisation is alive)

---

### 7.2 About

**Job:** Establish institutional legitimacy and historical depth.

**Structure:**
1. **Mission & Vision** — Concise, dignified statement
2. **History & Founding** — Timeline from 1983 with founding leaders named
3. **Governance** — How the IDU operates (Executive Committee, Secretary General, etc.)
4. **Honorary Advisory Board** — Full list with photos/bios; these are prestige names
5. **Sub-organisations** — Overview with links to each
6. **Values** — Democracy, freedom, rule of law — stated clearly
7. **IYDU sub-page** — Youth wing with own identity but IDU branding

**Key decisions:**
- The founding story is a primary trust signal — Thatcher/Reagan/Kohl founding this in 1983 is remarkable history. Tell it properly.
- Advisory Board photos matter — these are often recognisable figures

---

### 7.3 Members

**Job:** Be the definitive reference for IDU membership; allow easy discovery.

**Structure:**
1. **Interactive world map** — Click a country/region to see members
2. **Filterable directory** — By continent, country, membership status
3. **Party cards** — Logo, party name, country, short description, link to party site
4. **Regional groupings** — Aligned with sub-organisations (Africa, Latin America, etc.)

**Key decisions:**
- This is one of the most-visited pages by researchers and media; accuracy and completeness are paramount
- Each party deserves equal dignity — logo + name minimum; ideally a brief description
- Filter/search is essential for 80+ parties

---

### 7.4 News

**Job:** Serve as the IDU's official record of statements, resolutions, and activities.

**Structure:**
1. **Filterable news feed** — By type (Statement, Resolution, Event Report, Award, Press Release)
2. **Featured/pinned** — Most significant recent item pinned at top
3. **Archive** — Accessible chronologically back to available records
4. **RSS feed** — For journalists and researchers who monitor by RSS

**Key decisions:**
- Category taxonomy matters: differentiate Statements (IDU official positions) from News (activities, events, awards)
- Dates must be prominent — this is a record, not a blog
- Share buttons focused on LinkedIn and X (institutional sharing platforms)

---

### 7.5 Events / Events Directory

**Job:** Communicate the IDU's ongoing convening power.

**Structure:**
1. **Upcoming events** — Featured, date-prominent, with location and brief description
2. **Events calendar** — Visual or list calendar view
3. **Past events archive** — With links to event reports/news items

**Key decisions:**
- Past events are as important as upcoming — they prove consistent activity
- Event imagery from past summits is powerful; archive pages should feature photography

---

### 7.6 Leadership

**Job:** Provide authoritative identification of IDU's current leaders.

**Structure:**
1. **Secretary General** — Bio, photo, statement
2. **Executive Committee** — Names, party affiliation, country, photo
3. **Honorary Officers** — If applicable
4. **Contact** — Secretary General's office contact details

**Key decisions:**
- Photos are required — an anonymous leadership page is a credibility gap
- Party affiliations and countries must be included — this is the IDU's proof of diversity

---

### 7.7 Media Gallery

**Upgrade to Media Centre:**

**Job:** Make journalists' lives easy; project professionalism.

**Structure:**
1. **Press Releases** — Downloadable PDFs, chronological
2. **Press Kit** — One-click download: IDU logo files, boilerplate text, leadership headshots, factsheet
3. **Photo Library** — Searchable/filterable high-res photography from events, award ceremonies, summits
4. **Video** — Embedded event videos if available
5. **Press Contact** — Name, email, and possibly phone — prominently displayed

**Key decisions:**
- Rename "Media Gallery" to "Media Centre" — the latter signals professionalism and completeness
- Press kit download must be one click from the homepage Media section
- Photo captions must include names, dates, events — journalists cannot use unattributed photos

---

### 7.8 Privacy Policy

Standard legal page. Ensure GDPR-compliant given European member parties and likely EU-based operations.

## 9. Technical Strategy (WordPress)

### 9.1 WordPress Implementation Notes

- **Theme:** Custom theme or heavily customised premium theme (Astra Pro, GeneratePress Pro, or custom). No out-of-the-box themes — IDU requires custom design.
- **Page Builder:** Avoid heavy page builders (Elementor causes performance issues). Prefer custom Gutenberg blocks or a lightweight system.
- **Members directory:** Custom Post Type for parties; filterable via JS (Isotope or FacetWP)
- **Events:** The Events Calendar plugin (industry standard) or custom CPT
- **News/Press:** Native WordPress posts with custom taxonomies for content type
- **Member Login:** If a portal is needed, consider WP-Members or custom login; ensure SSL throughout
- **Search:** SearchWP or Relevanssi for better institutional-grade search

### 9.2 Performance

- Target: <2.5s LCP (Core Web Vitals)
- Image optimisation: WebP format, lazy loading, CDN
- Hosting: Ensure server is enterprise-grade (not shared hosting) — institutional sites need reliability

### 9.3 Accessibility

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum — IDU's audiences include government bodies in jurisdictions with accessibility mandates
- Keyboard navigable, screen-reader compatible
- Sufficient colour contrast across blues palette

- "International Democracy Union"
- "Centre-right political parties international"
- "Bush-Thatcher Award"

### 10.2 Structured Data

Implement:

### 10.3 Entity Authority

## 11. Content Strategy

### 11.1 Content Principles

1. **Institutional voice, not marketing voice** — State facts; let the weight speak
2. **Recency signals are critical** — Outdated content is an institutional credibility crisis
3. **Accuracy over completeness** — Better to have 75 correctly described members than 85 with errors
4. **Historical depth is an asset** — 40+ years of history should be documented, not hidden

### 11.2 Content Gaps to Address

Based on the typical IDU site audit:

| Gap | Risk | Priority |
|-----|------|----------|
| Outdated member list | Credibility damage with researchers | High |
| Missing/old leadership photos | Professionalism signal failure | High |
| No downloadable press kit | Journalist friction | High |
| No resolutions archive | Researcher barrier | Medium |
| Sub-org pages are thin | Undersells IDU's global scope | Medium |
| Founding history underdeveloped | Misses primary trust signal | Medium |

### 11.3 Content Governance

Recommend establishing clear responsibility for:
- News publishing (who approves, who posts, minimum frequency)
- Member directory updates (who maintains, review cadence)
- Events calendar maintenance
- Press contact availability

**Minimum content cadence:** At least 2 news items per month. A dormant news feed is catastrophic for institutional credibility.

## 15. Key Strategic Decisions Summary

1. **Positioning:** "The world's only global alliance of centre-right democratic parties" — factual, unambiguous, powerful
2. **Tone benchmark:** NATO/Council of Europe calibre — not EPP campaign energy
3. **Homepage anchor:** Founding names (Thatcher, Reagan, Kohl) appear above the fold — the primary trust signal
4. **Media Gallery → Media Centre:** Upgrade to serve journalists properly with press kit, press contacts, and dated releases
5. **Members page:** Interactive, filterable, logo-forward — the IDU's most-used page by external audiences
6. **News cadence:** Minimum 2/month — dormant news = perception of defunct institution
7. **Sub-organisations:** Elevated from footnotes to featured content — they prove global infrastructure
8. **Bush-Thatcher Award:** Featured prominently as prestige signal — two of the most recognisable names in democratic history
9. **No member recruitment framing:** IDU does not solicit members publicly; the site convenes, informs, and confers
10. **Phased delivery:** 16-week build in 3 phases; Members directory accuracy is a gate before launch
