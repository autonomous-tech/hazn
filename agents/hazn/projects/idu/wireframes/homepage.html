About the IDU

The World's Premier Centre-Right Democratic Alliance

The International Democracy Union is a working association of political parties of the centre and centre-right. Founded in London in 1983 by Margaret Thatcher, Ronald Reagan, and Helmut Kohl, the IDU brings together like-minded parties from across the globe.

With over 80 member parties representing millions of voters across six continents, the IDU coordinates policy, provides election assistance, and champions the cause of liberal democracy worldwide.

Named for President George H.W. Bush and Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher — two titans of democratic governance.
