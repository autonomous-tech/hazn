International Democracy Union

About the IDU

Forty years of uniting democrats across every continent in the common cause of freedom.

Our Mission

"To promote democracy, freedom, and the rule of law, and to advance the principles of centre-right governance as the foundation of peaceful, prosperous, and just societies."

The IDU exists as a forum in which political parties holding similar beliefs can come together and assist each other in a variety of political activities. The IDU facilitates the exchange of views on matters of common interest, helps with election campaigns, and promotes the development of democratic parties and institutions in new and emerging democracies.

History

Four Decades of Democratic Leadership

Founding in London

Margaret Thatcher, Ronald Reagan, Helmut Kohl, and leaders from 18 democratic parties gather in London to sign the founding declaration of the International Democracy Union. The IDU is born as the premier alliance of centre-right parties.

1983

London, United Kingdom

Post-Cold War Expansion

1990s

Democracy Across Continents

Following the fall of communism, the IDU expands rapidly across Central and Eastern Europe. New member parties from Poland, Czech Republic, Hungary, and the Baltic states join the alliance. The IDU begins providing election observation and party-building support.

Global Reach Established

The IDU establishes regional affiliates: APDU for Asia-Pacific, UPLA for Latin America, and DUA for Africa. Membership surpasses 60 parties. The Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom is inaugurated to recognise outstanding contributions to democracy.

2000s

Worldwide

Present Day

2024

80+ Parties, 6 Continents

The IDU today represents over 80 member parties across six continents, including parties of government in the United States, United Kingdom, Germany, Australia, Canada, Israel, and Japan. The IDU continues to be the world's most influential centre-right international.

Governance

How the IDU is Governed

01

Leaders Meeting

The supreme governing body, convened every two years, bringing together heads of member parties to set the IDU's strategic direction.

02

Executive Committee

The governing council between Leaders Meetings, responsible for day-to-day oversight. Meets quarterly. Includes representatives from all regional affiliates.

03

Secretariat

Based in Geneva, the professional Secretariat manages day-to-day operations, coordinates member relations, and delivers IDU programmes and publications.

Our Values

The Principles That Unite Us

🗳️

Democracy

Free and fair elections, representative government, and the peaceful transfer of power are the cornerstones of our political philosophy.

🕊️

Freedom

Individual liberty — of speech, press, conscience, and economic activity — is the bedrock on which free societies and free markets are built.

⚖️

Rule of Law

No one is above the law. The independence of the judiciary and respect for constitutionally established norms is non-negotiable.

🌍

Human Rights

Universal human rights are not a gift of government but an inherent dignity of every person. The IDU defends these rights unconditionally.
