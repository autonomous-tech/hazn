About the Award

Named for Two Titans of Democratic Governance

The Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom is named in honour of two of the twentieth century's greatest champions of liberty: President George H.W. Bush and Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher — both founding architects of the International Democracy Union and tireless defenders of democratic governance.

The award is conferred annually upon an individual or organisation that has demonstrated exceptional courage, leadership, and commitment to the promotion of freedom, democracy, the rule of law, and human rights — often in the face of authoritarian opposition or at great personal risk.

Recipients are nominated by IDU member parties and selected by the Executive Committee. The award is presented at the IDU's flagship annual conference.

Republic of Belarus

2024
Award Year

2024 Bush-Thatcher Award for Freedom

"For her extraordinary courage and unwavering resolve in leading the democratic movement of Belarus in the face of Alexander Lukashenko's brutal authoritarian regime."

Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya has become the most prominent symbol of Belarus's democratic aspirations following the disputed 2020 presidential election. Despite living in exile under constant threat, she has led a sustained international campaign for free elections, the release of political prisoners, and the restoration of democratic governance in Belarus.

The IDU Executive Committee selected Ms. Tsikhanouskaya unanimously, citing her extraordinary personal sacrifice and the moral clarity of her democratic leadership as exemplary of the values for which the Award stands.

Nominations

Nominate a Champion of Freedom

Nominations for the Bush-Thatcher Award are open to all IDU member parties. The nomination period opens each January. Nominees must be individuals or organisations that have made an outstanding contribution to democracy, freedom, or human rights within the preceding two years.
