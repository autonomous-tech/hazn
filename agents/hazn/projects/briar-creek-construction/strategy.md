# Website Strategy — Briar Creek Construction

---

## Positioning Statement

Briar Creek Construction is Ohio's trusted residential general contractor — delivering custom new builds, whole-home remodels, and seamless additions with the craftsmanship and communication that homeowners deserve. We combine the accountability of a local company with the capability of a full-service GC, so Ohio families get it done right the first time.

---

## Target Audience

- **Primary:** Ohio homeowners aged 35–65, household income $80K+, planning a significant home project (new build, major remodel, or addition). They've done some research, they're cautious about who they hire, and they want a contractor they can trust — not the cheapest bid. They make decisions jointly as a couple. Decision timeline: 1–6 months.

- **Secondary:** Real estate investors and property developers doing residential renovations or new construction in Ohio. More transactional, faster decision cycle, value efficiency and reliability.

- **Anti-persona:** Renters, DIYers looking for advice, people seeking cosmetic handyman-level work (painting, minor repairs), or homeowners looking only for the lowest possible price with no regard for quality. These visitors consume support resources without converting.

---

## Value Proposition

- **Headline:** Built Right. Built for Ohio.
- **Subhead:** Full-service residential construction — new homes, remodels, and additions — by a contractor your neighbors already trust.

- **Proof points:**
  - ✅ Licensed & insured Ohio residential general contractor — we handle every trade, start to finish
  - ✅ Transparent project communication — you always know what's happening and when
  - ✅ Local roots, long-term reputation — we build in communities where we live
  - ✅ On-time, on-budget commitment with documented project milestones
  - ✅ Real photos of real Ohio projects — no stock imagery, no bait-and-switch

## Conversion Strategy

- **Primary CTA:** Call Now — (555) 000-0000 — prominent on every page, sticky header on mobile, repeated at section breaks. Framed as "Get a Free Estimate" to reduce friction.

- **Secondary CTA:** "Request a Callback" form (name, phone, project type, timeline) — for visitors not ready to call. Low-commitment entry point that captures lead data.

- **Trust signals needed:**
  - Ohio contractor license number displayed in footer
  - Insurance/bonding badge
  - Years in business / projects completed counter
  - Before/after project photo galleries (real work, named by location e.g. "Dublin Addition" or "Westerville Kitchen Remodel")
  - Google Reviews widget or star rating with review count
  - BBB accreditation badge (if applicable)
  - Named testimonials with first name, city, and project type
  - Professional headshot(s) of owner/team — faces build trust
  - "Locally Owned & Operated" callout

## Content Requirements

- **Pages needed:**
  1. **Home** — Hero with primary CTA, services overview, trust signals, featured project, testimonials, final CTA
  2. **Services** — Overview page linking to service-specific pages
  3. **New Home Construction** — Process, what's included, Ohio market context
  4. **Remodeling** — Kitchen, bathroom, whole-home, basement finish
  5. **Additions** — Room additions, second-story additions, garage additions
  6. **Our Work / Gallery** — Project portfolio organized by type/location
  7. **About Us** — Story, team, values, community ties
  8. **Testimonials / Reviews** — Dedicated social proof page
  9. **Contact / Free Estimate** — Form + phone + service area map
  10. **Service Areas** — List of Ohio counties/cities served (SEO value)
  11. *(Optional)* **FAQ** — Common homeowner questions about the building/remodeling process

- **Key topics:**
  - What does a residential GC do? (educate + position)
  - How to choose a contractor in Ohio
  - What the construction/remodel process looks like step by step
  - Cost ranges for common projects (sets expectations, builds trust)
  - Ohio building permits and what homeowners need to know
  - Local project spotlights (SEO + social proof)

- **Social proof:**
  - Project case studies: before photo → challenge → process → after photo → homeowner quote
  - Google Reviews embedded or screenshot carousel
  - Video testimonials if available
  - "X projects completed in Ohio" running counter
  - Named testimonials sorted by project type so visitors self-identify

## Brand Voice

- **Tone:** Straight-talking, confident, warm. The voice of a skilled craftsman who respects your home and your investment. Not salesy. Not corporate. Midwestern directness with genuine pride in the work.

- **Words to use:**
  - Honest, reliable, skilled, local, trusted, quality, craftsmanship, your home, we handle it, peace of mind, straightforward, licensed, experienced, Ohio, neighbors, family, built to last, on your timeline

- **Words to avoid:**
  - World-class, cutting-edge, innovative, synergy, solutions, leverage, seamless (overused), affordable (implies cheap), cheap, discount, fast (implies rushed), guaranteed perfection, we're the best

## Success Metrics

- **Primary:**
  - Phone call conversions (tracked via call tracking number, Google Ads call extensions)
  - Form submissions (contact/estimate requests)
  - Cost per lead from organic and paid traffic

- **Secondary:**
  - Organic search rankings for "[city] home builder," "[city] remodeling contractor," "home addition Ohio" etc.
  - Time on site and pages per session (engagement quality)
  - Gallery page views (indicates project interest)
  - Google Business Profile call clicks and direction requests
  - Return visitor rate (indicates consideration phase)

## Strategic Notes for Downstream Agents

**For UX Architect:**
- Hero section must include a strong visual (real project photo), headline, subhead, and TWO CTAs (call + form). Mobile-first — call button must be thumb-accessible.
- Trust bar (license, insurance, years in business, review count) should appear above the fold or immediately below hero.
- Gallery is a high-priority conversion asset — design it to showcase before/after and project type filtering.

**For Copywriter:**
- Lead with outcomes for homeowners, not process. "Your dream kitchen" before "our 12-step process."
- Every page needs a unique CTA section — don't repeat the same copy verbatim.
- Service pages should answer: What is it? Who is it for? What does it cost/involve? Why us?

**For Developer:**
- Image performance is critical — large photo-heavy site. Implement lazy loading, next-gen formats (WebP/AVIF), and CDN delivery.
- Call tracking number should be swappable via environment variable for future analytics integration.
- Schema markup: LocalBusiness, Contractor, Review aggregates for SEO.
- Service area pages should be templated for easy replication across Ohio cities.
