## Technical Lesson Example

**Pillar:** Attribution  
**Mood:** Confident/helpful

```
Most agencies set up attribution wrong.

Here's the pattern I see over and over:

1. They pick a tool (Triple Whale, Northbeam, whatever)
2. They install it
3. They look at the dashboard
4. They trust the numbers

That's not attribution. That's faith.

Real attribution requires:
→ Understanding the model the tool uses
→ Comparing it to other sources (GA4, platform data)
→ Knowing when it's lying to you
→ Building triangulation, not single-source truth

The tool is 20% of the work.
The thinking is 80%.

Most agencies skip the thinking.
Don't be most agencies.
```

## Hot Take Example

**Pillar:** AI Agents  
**Mood:** Energetic/contrarian

```
Hot take: 90% of "AI agents" being sold to agencies are glorified chatbots.

I say this as someone who builds AI agents for a living.

A real agent:
- Takes autonomous action
- Handles multi-step workflows
- Integrates with your actual tools
- Fails gracefully when it can't

A chatbot with a system prompt:
- Answers questions
- That's it

Before you buy an "AI agent" for your agency, ask:
What does it DO when I'm not looking?

If the answer is "nothing" — it's a chatbot.
Nothing wrong with chatbots.
Just don't pay agent prices.
```

# Notes on Voice

**When writing for Rizwan:**
- He uses "—" dashes a lot for pauses
- Short sentences. Punchy.
- Sometimes one word. Paragraphs.
- Okay with fragments
- Uses "real talk" / "honest answer" / "here's the thing"
- Comfortable with uncertainty ("I don't know" / "I'm figuring it out")
- Uses lowercase for casual emphasis (not ALL CAPS usually)
- Ends with a thought, not always a CTA

**Avoid:**
- Emojis overload (occasional is fine)
- "Let me tell you..." openings
- Numbered lists with explanations for everything (sometimes just the list is enough)
- Asking for engagement explicitly
