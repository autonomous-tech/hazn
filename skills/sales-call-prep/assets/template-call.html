Tonight's Call

Raj Dubey — 30min Guide

VP of Development, LT Agency. Cold meeting via LinkedIn outreach. He's looking for GA4 help — that's your entry point.

Goal Learn & Position
Talk Ratio Him 70% / You 30%
Outcome Free Audit → Next Call
⚡ 3 Rules for Tonight
  • Ask, don't pitch. Every time you want to talk about yourself, ask a question instead.
  • Follow-up everything. "Tell me more about that." "What happens then?" "How does that work?"
  • Mirror his language. If he says "tracking" don't say "measurement infrastructure." Match him.
  • Take notes. Write down every pain point. You'll use them in the follow-up.

🟢 Open — Build Rapport (0:00–3:00)

3 minutes
Warm Up — Don't Pitch Yet
"Raj, appreciate you making the time. Before I get into anything, I'd love to hear a bit about you — how long have you been at LT, and what does your day-to-day look like?"

LISTEN Let him talk. He might mention team size, challenges, recent projects. Note everything.


If he asks "what do you guys do?" keep it to 15 seconds:

"We're a martech and analytics engineering team. We work with agencies as their backend partner for measurement, attribution, and data infrastructure. But I want to understand your world first — that'll tell me if there's even a fit."

🔵 Discover — Understand Their World (3:00–18:00)

This is 50% of the call. Don't rush it. Go deep on every answer.

Start with GA4 — His Intent Signal
"I saw there was interest around GA4. What's the situation there — is that for a specific client, or is it something across the board?"

LISTEN This reveals: scope of need, specific client, and whether it's an audit or implementation.


Follow-ups:

  • "What's currently set up? Are you on UA still or migrated?"
  • "Who manages the analytics day-to-day — your team, the client, or a third party?"
  • "What's the biggest frustration with the current setup?"
Understand Their Stack & Process
"Walk me through how a typical analytics or tracking project works at LT. Client asks for something — then what happens?"

LISTEN You're looking for: bottlenecks, handoff gaps, who does what.


Follow-ups:

  • "How much of your dev team's time goes to tracking vs. building product?"
  • "Do you have dedicated analytics people, or does the dev team handle it?"
  • "What tools are you guys running? GTM? Segment? Any CDPs?"
Find the Pain
"What's the hardest part about delivering analytics and measurement work for your clients?"

LISTEN This is the golden question. Whatever he says = your pitch angle later.


Follow-ups based on his answer:

  • If "bandwidth": "How often does tracking work get deprioritized because the team is on a bigger build?"
  • If "expertise": "What happens when you need server-side tracking or CAPI and your team hasn't done it before?"
  • If "client expectations": "Do clients ever ask for attribution or measurement that's hard to deliver?"
  • If "tooling": "Are you locked into tools you'd rather move off of?"
Understand the Business Context
"How does the analytics/martech work fit into LT's business model? Is it a standalone service line, or bundled into bigger engagements?"

NOTE This tells you how they price it and where the margin opportunity is.


Follow-ups:

  • "Do you bill analytics work separately, or is it part of a retainer?"
  • "Have you ever turned down analytics work because it wasn't worth the effort for your team?"
  • "What verticals have the most complex measurement needs?"

🟣 Position — Connect Your Value (18:00–24:00)

6 minutes
Mirror Back, Then Position

SAY Use his own words. Start with what you heard:

"So if I'm hearing you right, [mirror his pain back]. That's actually exactly the kind of problem we solve every day."

Then your 60-second pitch (adapt based on what he said):

"Here's what we do in a nutshell — we're a measurement and analytics engineering team. I've been in this space for 15 years. Started as a data modeler, became a solutions architect at Visier, and for the last four years I've been running Autonomous focused specifically on martech infrastructure and analytics measurement.

We work with agencies as their backend analytics partner. The stuff that's too specialized for a generalist dev team and too technical for a strategist — measurement architecture, attribution, server-side tracking, data pipelines, composable stacks. We handle the plumbing so your team can focus on the high-value builds.

And we do it white-label — your brand, your client relationship. We're invisible."

Key phrases to weave in (based on what resonates):

  • "Composable martech stack" — tools that don't lock you in
  • "We measure what matters, then build the infrastructure to capture it"
  • "Audit → Roadmap → Implementation — we handle the full lifecycle"
  • "We've done this for clients managing $50M+ in ad spend"
⚠️ Don't over-sell

You're not closing tonight. You're planting seeds. If you've done the discovery right, he's already connecting the dots himself. Less is more here.

🔶 Offer — The Free Audit (24:00–28:00)

4 minutes
Zero-Risk Next Step
"Here's what I'd like to do — and there's zero cost or commitment on your end. Let me run a MarTech Stack Blueprint on one of your clients. I'll audit their analytics setup, measurement infrastructure, and tracking health. You'll get a white-label PDF you can use in your next client meeting — either as an upsell or just to show you're on top of their data.

If the findings are valuable, we talk about next steps. If not, no hard feelings. Either way, you walk away with ammunition."

If he's interested:

  • "Which client would be the best fit? Maybe one of the casino or healthcare accounts where tracking is complex?"
  • "Can you share GA4/GTM access for that property? I'll have the Blueprint back in 3-5 business days."

If he's hesitant:

"Totally fair. How about this — send me any GA4 property ID, even an internal one. I'll do a surface-level audit just from what's publicly observable and send you a sample of what the full Blueprint looks like. No access needed."

✅ Close — Lock Next Step (28:00–30:00)

2 minutes
Confirm & Schedule
"Raj, this was really helpful — I appreciate you walking me through how things work at LT. Let me put together [the Blueprint / a summary of what we discussed] and I'll send it over by [day]. Can we grab 15 minutes next week to walk through it?"

Lock it down:

  • Get a specific date for the next call
  • Get his email for the follow-up
  • If he offered a client for the audit, confirm which one
  • Send a calendar invite within 1 hour of hanging up

✅ DO

  • Take notes on pain points
  • Mirror his language back
  • Keep your pitch under 60 seconds
  • Offer the free audit
  • Lock a next step before hanging up

  • Name-drop clients (NDA)
  • Pitch services he hasn't expressed need for
  • Rush the discovery section
  • Try to close a deal tonight
  • Say "we can do everything" — be specific
