# Document Structure Guide

## Strategy Doc Sections

### 1. Hero
- Partnership label + company names
- One-line value proposition
- Meta: target revenue, risk level, entry point, timeline

### 2. Executive Summary
- The Opportunity (what they are, the gap)
- The Play (how we fit)
- Risk Level with context

### 3. Key Numbers
- 3-column stat blocks (revenue, employees, team size, etc.)

### 4. Company Overview
- 2-column cards: prospect company + sister/parent companies
- Key clients grouped by vertical (3-column grid)

### 5. The Gap Analysis
- Callout box with core insight
- 3-column comparison: What they say → What happens → What we do

### 6. Service Opportunities
- Table: Service | Our Cost | Price to Them | They Bill Client | Fit Level
- Pricing strategy callout (don't price too low — signal expertise)

### 7. Financial Impact
- Side-by-side: Current State vs With Us
- Lead with THEIR savings/freed capacity, not our revenue
- Big stat blocks for the "wow" numbers

### 8. Engagement Plan
- Phased cards with badges (Phase 1/2/3)
- Phase 1: Prove value (free audit, first project)
- Phase 2: Scale (retainer, expanded services)
- Phase 3: Strategic partner (revenue share, joint proposals)

### 9. Objection Handling
- Card per objection with title + italicized response quote
- 5-6 common objections relevant to the prospect

### 10. Risk Factors
- Table: Risk | Likelihood (status pill) | Mitigation
- Include at least one "new" risk not in the original research

### 11. First Meeting Script (if included in strategy doc)
- Discovery questions, positioning script, offer, close

### 12. Bottom Line
- Hero-style closing section
- Year 1/2 targets, entry cost

## Call Guide Sections

### 1. Hero
- Contact name, title, company
- Meta: goal, talk ratio target, desired outcome

### 2. Rules Box
- 3-4 rules (callout box, grid layout)
- e.g., Ask don't pitch, Mirror language, Follow-up everything

### 3. Time Blocks (scale to call length)

#### Open Block (10% of time)
- Warm-up question
- 15-second elevator pitch if they ask

#### Discover Block (50% of time) — MOST IMPORTANT
- Start with their intent signal (why they took the meeting)
- Stack/process questions (how things work today)
- Pain questions (what's hardest, what breaks)
- Business context (how it fits their model/revenue)
- Each question has follow-ups for different answers
- Tags: LISTEN, SAY, NOTE

#### Position Block (20% of time)
- Mirror back what you heard
- 60-second pitch (adapt to their pain)
- Key phrases to weave in
- "Don't over-sell" warning callout

#### Offer Block (13% of time)
- Zero-risk next step script
- If interested: specific asks (access, client choice, timeline)
- If hesitant: lower-barrier alternative

#### Close Block (7% of time)
- Confirm + schedule next call
- Get email, confirm deliverable, send calendar invite same day

### 4. Cheat Sheet
- 2-column DO / DON'T grid
- "If you forget everything else" — one golden question

### 5. Credibility Bullets
- Background, years of experience, methodology, tools, social proof
- "Use sparingly" note

## Tone & Style

- Professional but direct — no fluff
- Scripts in quote blocks — ready to read verbatim if needed
- Use color-coded tags (LISTEN=cyan, SAY=green, NOTE=amber, CLOSE=purple)
- Every section should be scannable in 10 seconds
- Mobile-friendly (prospect might read on phone)
