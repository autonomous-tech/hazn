# Discovery Question Frameworks

## Universal Framework (works for any prospect)

### Situation (understand their world)
- "Walk me through how [relevant process] works at [company]."
- "What does your team structure look like?"
- "What tools/platforms are you running today?"
- "How long have you been doing it this way?"

### Problem (find the pain)
- "What's the hardest part about [their challenge]?"
- "Have you ever had to turn down work because [bandwidth/expertise]?"
- "When [trigger event] happens, what do you do?"
- "What keeps you up at night about [their area]?"

### Implication (make pain feel urgent)
- "How much time does your team spend on [low-value task]?"
- "What happens when [pain point] isn't addressed?"
- "How does that affect your client relationships?"
- "Have you lost revenue because of [gap]?"

### Need-Payoff (let them describe the solution)
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would this look like?"
- "What would it mean for your team if [pain] was solved?"
- "If I could take one thing off your plate tomorrow, what would it be?"

## Follow-Up Power Phrases
- "Tell me more about that."
- "What do you mean by [their word]?"
- "How does that work exactly?"
- "What happens then?"
- "And when that happens, what do you do?"
- "How long has that been an issue?"
- "Has anyone tried to fix that before?"

## By Meeting Type

### Cold Meeting (first time, no relationship)
Priority: Build rapport → Understand → Position lightly → Offer free value
- Spend 60% on discovery
- Keep pitch under 60 seconds
- Always offer something free
- Lock next meeting before hanging up

### Warm Intro (referral or existing relationship)
Priority: Acknowledge connection → Go deeper faster → Be specific
- Less rapport needed, go to discovery faster
- Can be more direct about services
- Reference the mutual connection's context

### Follow-Up (second+ call)
Priority: Recap → Deliver → Expand → Close
- "Last time you mentioned [pain]. Here's what we found."
- Present deliverable (audit, blueprint)
- Expand scope: "We also noticed [new finding]"
- Close on specific engagement

## Qualifying Questions (determine fit before investing time)
- "When you need outside help, what does the approval process look like?"
- "Have you worked with external partners before? How did that go?"
- "What would make a partnership like this a no-brainer for you?"
- "What's your timeline for addressing [the pain]?"
- "Is there budget allocated for this, or would we need to build a case?"

## Industry-Specific: Marketing Agencies
- "How much of your dev team's time goes to [martech/tracking] vs product?"
- "When [strategist/consultant] recommends something, who executes?"
- "Do you bill [analytics/martech] work separately or bundle it?"
- "What verticals have the most complex [measurement/tracking] needs?"
- "Have you ever scoped martech work into a proposal and then struggled to deliver?"

## Industry-Specific: SaaS / Tech
- "What does your data pipeline look like today?"
- "How do you measure [feature adoption / attribution / conversion]?"
- "Who owns analytics — product, engineering, or a dedicated team?"
- "What's your biggest data blind spot?"

## Industry-Specific: E-commerce / DTC
- "How are you tracking customer journey across channels?"
- "What's your attribution model? How confident are you in it?"
- "How do email/SMS and paid work together in your stack?"
- "What's your CAC and how are you measuring it?"
