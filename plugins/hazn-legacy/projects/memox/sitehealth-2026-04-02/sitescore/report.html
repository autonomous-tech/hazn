Executive Summary

Strong on-site fundamentals, invisible off-site

Memox.io has excellent technical SEO and schema markup — one of the best structured data implementations we've audited. However, blog content is blocked from search engines, organic visibility is near-zero outside branded searches, and off-site entity presence is effectively non-existent. These three issues compound: search engines and AI platforms have no content to index and no third-party signals to validate the brand.

High Priority

Blog content blocked by robots.txt Observed

Disallow: /blog/ appears for every user agent. Meanwhile, 15 insight articles are listed in sitemap.xml (under /insights/). The /blog/ disallow may be an artifact from a content restructure — the live content lives at /insights/ which is not blocked. However, the /products/ and /category/ disallow rules may also be blocking legitimate content paths. Recommendation: Audit which paths under /products/, /ctg/, /category/, and /blog/ should actually be blocked vs. indexed. Remove the /blog/ rule if that content has been migrated to /insights/.

Medium Priority

No explicit canonical tags Observed

The homepage HTML has no <link rel="canonical"> tag. While og:url is set to https://memox.io, explicit canonical tags are the SEO standard for deduplication. Without them, search engines may index duplicate versions (www vs non-www, trailing slash variants). Recommendation: Add self-referencing canonical tags to every page via Next.js metadata.
