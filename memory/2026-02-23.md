**Your BATNA:** Walk away. Keep 100%, find other clients. He gets 0%.
**His BATNA:** 0%. No deal = no commission. 25% of Y1 >> nothing.

---

**1. Accusation Audit (Open with this)**
> "I want to make sure we get this right, so let me start by saying — I know this might feel like I'm changing the terms. You might think I'm being difficult, or that I don't value what you brought to the table. You probably feel like you earned this, and now I'm trying to claw it back."

*Pause. Get "That's right" (not "You're right").*

---

**2. Label His Position (Tactical Empathy)**
> "It sounds like you see this as a long-term income stream. It seems like you feel the intro is worth ongoing value."

Then pivot:
> "Help me understand — how does the ongoing commission connect to ongoing work?"

---

**3. Calibrated "How" Questions (Make him solve your problem)**
> "How am I supposed to pay 20% ongoing when my margins are less than that after delivery costs?"
> "How does it work when I'm the one doing the renewals, the relationship management, the upsells — and the commission stays the same year after year?"
> "What am I paying for in year 3?"

*Silence. Let him sit in the problem.*

---

**4. Reframe: Rent Collector → Growth Partner**
> "What if we structure this so you're not capped — but you're paid for results, not just the intro?"
> "You get 25% of year one. That's real money. Any upsell, expansion, growth you help create — 15-20% of that new revenue."
> "More clients you bring? 25% Y1 on each. No ceiling."
> "But I can't pay ongoing commission on revenue I'm earning through my own work."

---

**5. The Offer**
| What | Commission |
|------|------------|
| Year 1 (Izdihar) | 25% — earned the intro |
| Year 2+ (same revenue) | 0% — you do the work |
| Upsell/expansion he helps | 15-20% of NEW revenue |
| New client referrals | 25% Y1 each, same structure |

---

**6. If He Pushes Back**

"No" technique:
> "Is it unreasonable to pay commission on work someone actually does?"

Loss aversion:
> "I'd hate for us to lose this over structure. 25% of $60k is $15,000 in your pocket this year. That's real. I don't want you walking away with nothing because we couldn't agree on year 3."

---

**7. If He Says "But I Brought the Client"**
> "You did. And 25% of year one reflects that. But in year two, year three — who's doing the work? Who's on the calls? Who's handling delivery? Who's earning the renewal?"
> "I can't pay for an intro forever. I can pay for contribution."

---

**8. The Walk-Away (calm, direct)**
> "I want to make this work. But I can't give away more than I keep. If we can't find something fair, I'd rather pass than build resentment into the relationship. No hard feelings."

*Mean it.*

---

**9. Black Swan — Find His Real Driver**
> "What's making the ongoing piece so important to you?"

Listen for: precedent, wrong margin assumptions, financial pressure, trust issues.

---

**Your Principles (non-negotiable):**
1. Ongoing money requires ongoing work
2. 25% Y1 is generous — don't let him frame it as lowball
3. Growth is unlimited if he keeps contributing
4. You'll walk. And he knows it
